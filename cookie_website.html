The Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookie

By Alton Brown

This is essentially a hack of the most famous cookie recipe in the world, which we all know from the back of the Nestle “morsel” bag. Extra chewiness is attained by substituting bread flour for regular all purpose, replacing one egg white with milk, and changing the ratio of brown to white sugar. I don't recommend half or double batches. Instead, make a whole batch, bake what you want, portion and freeze the rest.

Keep in mind this is a “chocolate chip” cookie and therefore the quality of the final product will be greatly effected by the quality of the chips used. Although the semi-sweet “morsels” that we all know from the yellow bag are serviceable, I'd suggest combining such kid-friendly fodder with something a bit more daring, say dark chocolate chips/chunks in the 68% cocoa range. Oh, and remember you can chop your own.

Makes about 2 dozen cookies.

  • Total time: 1 hour 50 minutes
  • Prep: 20 min
  • Active time: 20 minutes
  • Inactive: 1 hour
  • Cook: 30 min

Cook's Note The darker the sugar you use, the chewier your cookies will be.
